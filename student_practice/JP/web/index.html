Exploring relationship between attention and the spatial environment
To establish a conducive environment for graditude jornaling, I've decided to turn my study table into a sanctury for writing gratitude thoughts. I sat at the newly organized study tbale for 30 minutes and wrote in my 'thankfulness diary'. The peaceful surrounding generated by the spatial internvention greatl improved my experience.
  • Increased focus: The calming environment helped me to quiet my mind and concentrate on the practice.
  • Enhanced emotional well-being: Expressing gratitude in a peaceful setting seemed to have a more profound impact on my mood and overall well-being.
  • Deepened connection: The physical space felt like a sacred place, fostering a deeper connection with myself and my thoughts.
  • Improved creativity: The aesthetic appeal of the space inspired creative journaling, leading to more detailed and heartfelt entries.

    9.25.2024
    Strange and strong affordances
    To re-invision and re-understand the objects and spaces through their affordances,not by their names,categories or class etc. The room has so many possibilities, so I chose some objects present in the surrounding place of my practice: Table; Chair; Lamp; Gratitude writing paper and Jar. where the affordances of each selected object is taken to point of absurdity where the table and chair are used in total opposite sense; The lamp is turned up-side-down where the one word thought is written in the shadow space of the inverted lamp and the 'Gratitude jar' is used from the outside where the gratitude thoughts written on chits are stuck on the outside of the jar to see their affordances at that point.
  • Distracted focus: The absurd environment wether it was the table I sat on or the inverted chair I wrote on felt uncomfortable which led to distracting my mind and concentration on the practice.
  • Multi connection: The physical space around started having multiple focus points due to the distractions, hence causing fragmented thoughts.
  • Materiality: Due to the absurd usage of the objects, the materials of the inverted objects felt quite different and altogether provided a new experience for my gratitude journaling.

    To envision an attentional artifact informed by the practice
    To Create a large, tactile dial that represents different states of attention. As the user turns the dial, various environmental factors change to support different attentional states.
  • Components
  • 1. Large, smooth-turning dial (controlled by a stepper motor)
  • 2. LED ring around the dial for visual feedback
  • 3. Speakers for audio cues
  • Functionality
  • 1. The dial has distinct "clicks" or positions, each representing a different attentional state (e.g., "Focused", "Open Awareness", "Rest", "Creative Flow")
  • 2. As the user turns the dial: The stepper motor provides haptic feedback; LEDs change color and intensity; Ambient lighting in the room shifts and Audio atmosphere adjusts (white noise, nature sounds, music)

the sun rises in the east and sets in the west.
is that all it does?
for 200,000 years, before iphones, before clocks, before watches,
the sun told us all we needed to know.
look around you. the sun, the moon, the direction of the wind,
the stars, the clouds, the rain, all communicate time and space.
they are the original clock, calendar, compass, and map.
they are the original timekeepers.
you measure them with your eyes, the tilt of your head,
you can tell time using the dimensions of your hands,
you can read the sky as easily as you read a clock face.
as intuitively as you use google maps.
and this is how people lived for thousands and thousands of years.
but now, we are lost.
can we relearn how to appreciate the sun?
by using the movements of our hands,
and our own interpretations and sensory experiences?
this tool will hopefully push you to always look up.

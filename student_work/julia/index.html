Wakefulness as Creative Balance

Julia Kwon

Description

My attentional theory views wakefulness as a dynamic interplay of physical balance and creative expression. Attention becomes a creative instrument, shaped by the conditions of the body and environment, fostering mindfulness and spontaneity. By replacing hurried routines with intentional engagement, my project transforms wakefulness into a practice of balance, exploration, and attunement to the present moment.

Attentional Theory

My relationship to attention, particularly in the context of wakefulness, is evolving from a reactive to a generative practice. Traditionally, my attention has been kickstarted by external stimulants like coffee or the lyrical drama of Erika’s arias, followed by the rush of daily demands. However, this project invites a transformation: to view wakefulness not as a task to be managed but as a state to be created. I see my attention as a balancing act infused with creativity. It is physical focus in action, a dynamic state of equilibrium shaped by both the conditions of my body and the external environment. On the balancing board, I am both the doer and the receiver: a composer of spontaneous sounds and a listener to the unique sounds that emerge. Each morning becomes an exploration of mindfulness and presence, sculpted by the interplay of movement, sound, and mood. The metaphor that best captures my attention is that of a creative instrument. Like a wire reshaped to produce unique tones, my attention shifts and adjusts according to the physical and emotional conditions of the moment. Through this interplay, I aim to replace the hurried monotony of coffee-fueled wakefulness with a practice of intentional engagement, one that listens to the rhythms of my body and encourages playful curiosity. My current relationship with attention reflects a need for intentional pauses. Whether its a morning ritual to center myself or a walk to recalibrate my focus during the day, I am learning to use motion and physical space to reset and ground my awareness. This project takes that practice further, creating a ritual of wakefulness that is not only functional but deeply personal and creatively expressive. Ultimately, my goal is to cultivate wakefulness as a state of mindful creativity. By listening to my body, responding to my environment, and allowing spontaneity to shape my mornings, I am fostering a deeper connection to my attention—one that encourages balance, exploration, and attunement to the present moment.
